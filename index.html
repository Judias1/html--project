    TYPES OF CREATURES

    PASSIVE

    Passive creatures are those that avoid conflict and remain non-aggressive, exhibiting little to no reaction unless directly provoked. They tend to coexist peacefully within their environments, often displaying behaviors that prioritize avoidance and tranquility over confrontation.


    NEUTRAL

    Neutral creatures maintain a balanced stance, not actively seeking out confrontation but remaining alert and willing to defend themselves if threatened. They often interact with their surroundings without strong aggression or passivity, responding based on the situation rather than exhibiting a fixed behavior.


    HOSTILE

    Hostile creatures exhibit aggressive behavior and actively seek to attack or intimidate others. They are quick to react with hostility in response to perceived threats or intrusions, often displaying fierce territoriality or defensive aggression. These creatures typically create a sense of danger in their environments, posing challenges to those who encounter them.
